Understanding Musical Notes

In music notation, different note symbols represent different durations of sound. Here's a simple breakdown of the most common types of notes:

Whole Note (ùÖù)

Also called a semibreve, it lasts for four beats in common time. It's an open (hollow) note head with no stem.

Half Note (ùÖû)

Also known as a minim, it lasts for two beats. It has an open note head and a stem.

Quarter Note (ùÖü)

Also called a crotchet, it lasts for one beat. It has a filled-in note head and a straight stem.

Eighth Note (ùÖ†)

Also called a quaver, it lasts for half a beat. It has a filled-in head, a stem, and one flag.

Sixteenth Note (ùÖ°)

Also called a semiquaver, it lasts for a quarter of a beat. It has a filled-in head, a stem, and two flags.

Thirty-Second Note (ùÖ¢)

Also called a demisemiquaver, it lasts for one-eighth of a beat. It has three flags on the stem.
